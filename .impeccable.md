# 星光回忆录 — Design Context

## Users
- **Who**: Everyday people (25-55) interviewing their elderly parents/grandparents to preserve family memories
- **Context**: Used at home, often in living rooms or during family gatherings. Emotional, intimate setting. Not tech-savvy users.
- **Job to be done**: Guide elders to share their life stories through gentle conversation, then compile those stories into a book
- **Emotional goals**: Warmth, nostalgia, trust, gentleness, respect for elders' stories

## Brand Personality
- **Voice**: 温暖、陪伴、珍藏 (Warm, Companion, Treasured)
- **3 words**: Warm, Reverent, Personal
- **Product name**: 星光回忆录 (Starlight Memoir) — each memory is like a star, together they form a constellation of a life

## Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Like an old family photo album meets warm evening light. Soft gradients, warm paper-like textures, gentle star-like accents
- **Theme**: Light default (family setting, daytime use), dark mode available (evening/bedtime reading feel)
- **Colors**: Warm amber/gold tones (like old photographs), soft cream backgrounds, deep navy for dark mode
- **Avoid**: Technical jargon in UI text, developer-centric labels (API, endpoint, ASR, TTS, Agent, ACP, STT), cold/clinical aesthetics
- **Reference feeling**: A well-loved family photo book, warm lamplight, handwritten letters

## Design Principles
1. **C-end first**: Every label should feel like it's talking to a person, not a developer. "录音" not "ASR", "朗读" not "TTS"
2. **Warm over technical**: Replace all API/endpoint/agent terminology with natural language in the UI
3. **Starlight theme**: Subtle star/sparkle motifs that reinforce the brand without being cheesy
4. **Respectful pacing**: The interface should feel unhurried — generous spacing, soft animations, patient empty states
5. **Chinese-first experience**: The product is primarily for Chinese users. Chinese text should feel native and beautiful, not translated
